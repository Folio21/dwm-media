# Cold Call Pitch Script
### Style: High Conviction | Pain-First | No Fluff

---

## Before You Call

Pull the lead from the tool. Know these before you dial:
- Business name
- Owner name (if you have it)
- Whether they have a website or not
- Their rating and review count

You are not calling to sell. You are calling to **expose a problem they already have.**

---

## The Open (First 10 Seconds — Make or Break)

> "Hey, is this [Owner Name]?"

*(Wait for yes.)*

> "Hey [Name], my name's David — I'll be straight with you, this is a cold call, but I promise it's worth 60 seconds of your time. Fair enough?"

**Why this works:** Disarms them. Nobody expects honesty. You're already different from every other caller they've ignored today.

---

## The Hook (The Pain)

**If they have NO website:**

> "I was looking up [Business Name] online — great reviews by the way, [X] stars is no joke. But here's the thing that caught my attention: you don't have a website. And right now, every single person in [City] who's Googling '[category] near me' — they're not finding you. They're finding your competitors. And some of those competitors have half your rating. That bother you at all?"

**If they have a website but it's outdated/bad:**

> "I was on your website earlier. I'm not going to sugarcoat it — it's not doing you any favors. People are judging your business in about three seconds on that page, and right now it's costing you customers before they ever pick up the phone. You know that feeling when you lose a job and you never even knew you were in the running? That's happening to you online every day."

**Why this works:** You're not pitching yet. You're holding up a mirror. People don't buy solutions — they buy relief from pain they've just been forced to feel.

---

## The Credibility Drop (Keep It Short)

> "I build websites specifically for local [category] businesses. Clean, fast, shows up on Google, built to get the phone ringing. Not some template that looks like everyone else's — something that actually makes someone choose you over the guy down the street."

---

## Handle the "I'm Busy" / "Not Interested" Early Brush-Off

> "I get it, you're running a business. I'm not here to waste your time — I'm here because I genuinely think you're leaving money on the table and I can fix it fast. Give me 30 more seconds and if it doesn't make sense, I'll let you go, no hard feelings."

---

## The Offer (Be Specific — Vague = Dead)

> "Here's what I do: I build professional websites specifically for local [category] businesses — full setup, your services, your photos, your contact info, built to show up on Google and get the phone ringing. One-time setup fee of $[X], then $[Y] a month for hosting and updates. Most clients make that back the first time a new customer finds them instead of a competitor."

**Then hit them with this:**

> "And listen — I didn't come into this call empty handed. I actually already built you a demo of what your site could look like. It's done. Right now. I can send you the link in the next two minutes and you can see exactly what I'm talking about. You like it, we move forward. You don't, you've lost nothing but two minutes. That work for you?"

**Why this works:** This is the nuclear close. They expected a pitch — you're handing them a finished product before they've paid a cent. The psychological shift is massive. It's no longer hypothetical. Their website already exists. They just have to say yes to own it.

---

## The Close (Assumptive — Don't Ask, Assume)

> "So let me ask you — what's a better time to send you that preview, later today or tomorrow morning?"

*(If they hesitate:)*

> "Look, I'm not asking you to commit to anything right now. I'm asking for the chance to show you what your business could look like online. If it doesn't blow you away, you move on. But if it does — and I think it will — you'll wish you'd done this two years ago."

---

## Objection Handlers

**"I don't have the budget right now."**
> "I hear that. But think about it this way — every month without a website is a month of customers going to someone else. This isn't a cost, it's an investment. And at $[Y]/month, we're talking about what, one or two new customers to break even? I think you get that in the first week."

**"I already have someone handling it."**
> "Perfect — so you know how important it is. All I'm asking is let me send you the preview, take 60 seconds to look at it, and if what I show you isn't better than what you have now, delete the email. Deal?"

**"Send me some information."**
> "I'll do you one better — instead of a generic brochure, I'll build you an actual preview of your site. Specific to your business, your location, your services. That way you're not looking at some pitch deck, you're looking at your actual business. What's the best email?"

**"I need to think about it."**
> "Totally fair. What's the one thing that's holding you back? Because usually when someone says that, there's one specific concern — and nine times out of ten I can address it right now."

---

## The Close (If They Say Yes to the Preview)

> "Perfect. I'll have your preview ready within 24 hours. When I send it over, take a look and let's jump on a quick call to walk through it together — I want to make sure it's exactly right before we do anything else. What's a good number to reach you on tomorrow?"

---

## Golden Rules

1. **Never pitch before you've established pain.** If they don't feel the problem, the solution means nothing.
2. **Confidence is non-negotiable.** You're not asking for a favor. You're offering them something they need.
3. **Silence is a weapon.** After you ask a closing question — shut up. The first person to speak loses.
4. **No means not yet.** Every objection is a question in disguise. Answer the question.
5. **You control the frame.** You're not a vendor. You're a specialist who found a problem in their business and is offering to fix it.

---

## One-Line Mindset Reset Before Every Call

*"They need this more than I need the sale."*
